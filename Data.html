The Rivers IMPACT project is concerned with malaria intervention through effective and efficient service delivery to serve all including the under-served and poor at the grass root to reduce malaria burden in the state. The Rivers State government has proactively put to action this commitment by accessing the world bank loan in collaboration with a Non-state Actor (ACOMIN) to effectively carry on the task to continuously lower the burden of malaria using a two-way approach of enabling environments of strong health systems strengthening with integration of malaria control, and multisectoral action for a swift reduction in malaria-related deaths through effective case management, availability of commodities and prevention methods in 319 wards across 23 LGA in Rivers state.

Significance of Impact Project

The significance of the Rivers malaria IMPACT project cannot be overemphasised and apart from strengthening the health system, capacity building and behavioural change, it is imperative that outcomes and concerns observed will be communicated to policy makers/government which should be acted upon promptly by giving it the appropriate response it deserves. Consequently, this will present a new opportunity for the populace to benefit. Furthermore, this is targeted at changing the narrative from high burden to less or no malaria burden in River State. The IMPACT project thus predicted an upsurge both in malaria prevention and control in the state due to effective health surveillance systems and the strategic use of data to track progress and scoring to triangulate as well as match commodity distribution, treatment and the impacts.
